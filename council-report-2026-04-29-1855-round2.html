Where the Council Clashes (R2)

Is the vendor + tracker combo an asset or a liability? The Contrarian sees liability — "trust inversion, discovery in lawsuits, regulators noticing" — and recommends shipping the app from a separate entity. The other four see the opposite: vertical integration is the moat. Resolution: the Contrarian's risk is real but mitigable. Don't cross-promote your peptide sales inside the app. Disclose the vendor relationship in About/legal. Don't claim the app makes dosing safer. If you do those three things, your liability is closer to a forum operator's than a pharmacy's.

Public virality vs captive audience for V1? Expansionist wants protocol-sharing QR codes from day 1. Outsider says "build for the 60 you have, scale is later." Resolution: ship V1 to your captive audience (60/500/150). Add protocol-sharing as V1.5 once those users are sticky.

What the Competitive Sweep Revealed (Chairman addition)

The space has 15+ direct competitors on the App Store / Play Store today. Two findings particularly matter:

  • TRT Plus already ships compound half-life charting with peak/steady-state/clearance visualization. That's exactly DoseTrace's planned "serum curve visualizer" V2 premium feature. You will not differentiate on this. Drop it from the roadmap or make it free as a check-the-box parity feature.
  • None of the 15 competitors are simultaneously a peptide vendor with 60 weekly buyers. That's a moat no app-store-only competitor can replicate. Your distribution is the single hardest thing they cannot copy.

Two specific feature gaps you have versus competitors that should move into V1.5 (not V2):

  • Injection site rotation + visual body map — TRT Tracker, OptiPin both have this. Cheap to build, expected by serious users.
  • Compound presets / one-tap setup — TRT Plus has 80+ pre-configured compounds. DoseTrace requires manual entry. For peptide noobs (your exact audience), one-tap "BPC-157 protocol" presets remove the biggest onboarding friction.

Round 2 Advisor Responses (full)
The ContrarianVendor + tracker = liability magnet. Ship from someone else.

The Fatal Flaw: he's selling two incompatible products simultaneously.

Evandro is a peptide vendor trying to launch a health tracker for peptide users. This creates three converging disasters:

1. Trust inversion. Users downloading his app will assume (correctly) he's collecting their protocol data to optimize his sales pitch and upsell them. Every bloodwork upload, every protocol they log — it's now business intelligence for his vendor operation. He can claim separation, but users won't believe it. Competitors will weaponize this. Regulators will notice.

2. Liability nightmare. The app currently tracks self-administered injections. If a user logs a protocol, gets injured, and sues — discovery reveals he's the vendor who sold them the peptide. He's now liable twice: as the seller AND as the tracker that didn't warn them. The app becomes a liability magnet.

3. Realistic conversion is 3-5%, not 20%. 60 weekly customers and a 500-list converting from "free tier" to "$50/yr paid" requires trust he doesn't have in this role. WhatsApp channels are notoriously low-intent. 10% opt-in × 30% paid conversion = 15 users from 500. That's a vanity metric, not a wedge.

4. The tirzepatide example reveals the problem. Tirzepatide is a prescription GLP-1. GLP-1 users are a totally different audience (insurance-covered, medical supervision). His actual peptide audience (epitalon, GHK-Cu, BPC-157) is grey-market, price-sensitive, trust-paranoid — and he IS the vendor. These are irreconcilable.

What he's missing: a business model that doesn't require him to be trustworthy. Sell the app anonymously or through a partner. Kill the vendor angle or kill the tracker. V1 should not ship from him. It should ship from someone else.

First PrinciplesReframe: this isn't a health app. It's a customer-retention tool.

Strip the noise: Evandro isn't building a health-tracking app. He's building a customer lock-in tool for his peptide business.

The question "should we add Apple Watch in V2?" is upside-down. The real question is "what's the minimum app that makes my 60 weekly buyers stickier and reduces support friction?"

Evandro sees a daily pain: customers ask "how do I reconstitute tirzepatide?" 50 times a month. That's not a feature gap — it's a support cost he can eliminate. The calculator isn't a feature; it's a deflection mechanism that reduces his operational burden.

The vial tracker, syringe guide, and reminders? Those are friction-reducers. They keep users from texting him "did I dose yesterday?" They keep him in the app while keeping buyers from switching vendors.

The 20-50k user goal isn't about app virality. It's about converting his 500-person list and leveraging his 150-member WhatsApp channel into app users. Distribution is solved.

V1 should be: a free tirzepatide (and common-stack) reconstitution calculator bundled with a simple dose log. That's it. Solves the named pain. Keeps users in the ecosystem. Minimal engineering. Ships in 2 weeks. 80% of the value at 5% of the scope.

The premium tier with "unlimited protocols" assumes he's selling to strangers. He's not. His margin is on peptide sales. The app is a loyalty mechanism. Multi-language? 500-person list. English first. Offline-first, voice reminders, barcode scanning — nice-to-haves at 20-50k users.

The product is not a health app. It's a customer service chatbot you don't have to maintain.

The ExpansionistV1 should be a platform, not a tracker. Vendor angle is a moat.

Ship NOW:

1. Reconstitution calculator as a free, standalone web tool — calc.dosetrace.io or a landing page widget. Becomes your SEO moat and top-of-funnel. Every peptide user Googling "tirzepatide reconstitution" lands here. Free, frictionless, category-defining.

2. Sharing protocol recipes — Users export/share exact stacks (5mg BPC-157 + 10mg TB-500 + X syringe) as a QR or link. Sharable, viral. Each share is a conversion funnel. V1 with 5+ ready-made templates from your vendor network.

3. Community + vendor integration from day 1 — Don't hide that you're a vendor. Lean into it. Users see "Suggested by [vendor]" for common stacks. This builds trust AND creates a network effect where other vendors want their stacks featured. You're building the protocol layer of the peptide internet.

4. Bloodwork correlation dashboard — Skip AI hype for V1. Ship a visual: "You increased TB-500 on Week 4, your WBC climbed 2 weeks later." Dead simple, no ML. Justifies premium and separates you from a generic tracker.

5. Intentionally sparse feature set — Lose the FAQ and blog aspirations. No "wellness content." One screen each: protocols, today's doses, bloodwork/correlations.

Defer to V2/V3: Causal inference / AI insights · Vitamin tracking (separate app) · 6-language i18n · RevenueCat complexity (just use Stripe) · Sync and offline-first.

The real opportunity: 20-50k users at $25-50 ARPU = $500k-$2.5M ARR — a great bootstrapped business. Your vendor relationships + user data = the infrastructure layer. In 18 months, you're selling anonymized stack recommendations to other vendors, telehealth partnerships, and supplement affiliate. The app is the Trojan horse; the data network is the moat.

The OutsiderOwn the vendor angle. Kill 6 languages. Kill vitamins.

You've built something more coherent than you know. The tirzepatide reconstitution pain point is real — that's not marketing, that's a genuine bottleneck your 60 weekly customers hit every time. But three concerning misdirections:

The vendor-as-tracker play is weirder than it needs to be. To a stranger, you're asking people to put their dose logs into an app built by the guy selling them the raw peptide powder. Not inherently disqualifying — actually a powerful distribution moat — but you're not leaning into it. You're pretending it's just a wellness app. Own the ecosystem angle. Right now it reads like you're embarrassed about the source.

The grey-market context is invisible to the app. Tirzepatide from powder reconstitution is legally murky — your 60-customer base knows this; your future 50k user base won't. The app needs to whisper "we know what you're really doing" without saying it. No shame. Just clarity. Your FAQ doesn't address this at all. It should.

Vitamins + peptides in one app is a feature tax, not a feature. A vitamin taker tracks "did I remember? do I feel better?" A tirzepatide user tracks "is my math right? how much is left?" Different UX problems wearing the same name. You've conflated distribution (your list has both) with product. Wrong. Ship V1 as the peptide tracker. Vitamins are 2027.

Six languages on day 1 is theatre. Your 60 core customers are English speakers. Your WhatsApp group runs in English. Shipping in 6 langs because you can dilutes the message. Launch in English. Make it sharp. Add Spanish in month 3 when you know what sucks.

What you're getting right: the app actually exists and your customers are waiting for it. That's worth way more than feature completeness. For V1 right now: Core dose logger + vial tracker + reconstitution math (don't touch). Keep simple streak/consistency view. Cut bloodwork PDFs, doctor PDFs, drug interaction, voice reminders, barcode scanning, Apple Watch, family profiles.

Stop building for the 20-50k user you hope exists in 5 years. Build for the 60 you have right now. Get them addicted to the core experience, then let them drag friends into the WhatsApp group. That's your real product.
