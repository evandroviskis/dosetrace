Three Corrections

1. The vendor-conflict argument was overstated

Affected advisor: The Contrarian · Affected verdict section: "Where the Council Clashes"
Council assumed
Evandro was building DoseTrace as an extension of his peptide-vending business — visible link, shared brand, cross-promotion inside the app. The Contrarian framed this as a "liability magnet" and "trust inversion."
What's actually true
DoseTrace is owned by Outcom, a separate legal entity. EvoxBiolabs (peptide sales) is not mentioned anywhere in the app, the website, or the company. The two businesses are operationally and legally independent. Evandro plans to exit peptide sales. The customer base is a launch springboard — not a permanent funnel.

Revised reading: the liability profile is closer to "indie founder cold-emails his existing network at launch" than to "pharmacy operates a clinical tracker." The Contrarian's underlying caution still has a residue (see "What still needs care" below), but the strategic case it argued against doesn't apply.

2. The serum curve visualizer IS differentiated

Affected advisor: Chairman (competitive sweep) · Affected verdict section: V4 plan + "What you're overthinking"
Council assumed
DoseTrace's planned "serum curve visualizer" was the same product TRT Plus already ships: a generic half-life chart from compound research data. Conclusion: drop it, you won't differentiate.
What's actually planned
The DoseTrace version overlays the user's actual bloodwork values on their own dosing timeline. Example: "Your testosterone was 850 ng/dL on Apr 15; you injected on Apr 8; here's YOUR personalized PK curve." That's bloodwork-grounded, not research-grounded. None of the surveyed competitors do this.

Revised reading: this is the single most defensible premium feature DoseTrace could build, because it's the only one that requires bloodwork uploads (which everyone else has) PLUS dose logs (which everyone else has) PLUS the math to fit a personal PK model. The combination is what makes it hard to copy. Reposition from "drop it" to "this is your premium hero in V2."

3. The 6-language launch is justified

Affected advisors: The Outsider, The Executor · Affected verdict section: "Where the Council Agrees" + V1 plan
Council assumed
Six languages on day 1 was "marketing theatre" for an English-speaking US audience. Recommended cutting to English (Outsider) or English + Spanish (Executor). Saved engineering time.
What's actually true
Brazil consumes peptides at a scale comparable to the US, including grey-market imports from Paraguay. Customers report discomfort discussing peptide dosing in non-native languages. The 6 chosen languages (EN, ES, PT, FR, DE, IT) map directly to Evandro's existing customer-nationality distribution. Influencer marketing in BRL/EUR markets costs 3.5–4.5× less than in USD — the localized markets aren't just optional, they're cheaper to acquire than the US market.

Revised reading: ship all 6 languages at V1. The translations exist, the cost to maintain them at this stage is marginal, and they unlock currency-leveraged distribution channels that the US-only frame missed entirely. The Outsider's "theatre" call was a cultural blind spot.

What still needs care (residual from the Contrarian's R2 critique)

Three risks that remain even with the legal separation

  1. The implicit link in your network. When you message your 150-member WhatsApp group "I built an app, check it out," the recipients know who you are. Even without a public corporate link, those users will associate "the peptide guy" with "the app guy." That's fine — but be deliberate about which hat you're wearing in each touchpoint. Don't blur them in messaging; don't use peptide-sale language to promote the app.
  2. Discovery in litigation. If a DoseTrace user is harmed and sues, discovery can still reach the founder's connected businesses regardless of corporate structure. The legal separation is real but not absolute. Standard mitigations (clear app disclaimers, no clinical claims, no "this app makes dosing safer" language, terms of service that limit liability) still matter.
  3. The exit timing question. If DoseTrace is the exit vehicle from peptide sales, the order of operations matters. Building DoseTrace credibility while still operating EvoxBiolabs creates a quiet phase where the link could surface. Plan the EvoxBiolabs wind-down explicitly — don't let it drift.

The One Thing to Do First (unchanged)

Today, before anything else: list the 20 most-asked compounds in your peptide vendor business. Tomorrow, build them as one-tap presets in the Protocols screen. That single feature will move install→active conversion more than anything else for your existing customers, and it remains the single biggest visible gap vs your competitors. The amendment doesn't change this.
